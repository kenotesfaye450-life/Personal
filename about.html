I'm Keno Tesfaye — a first-year Software Engineering student at Haramaya University with a deep conviction that technology is best understood from its foundations, not its surface. I don't just want to know what something does; I want to understand why it works the way it does.

My journey into technology began with a genuine fascination for how systems think. Whether it's the logic flow of a C++ program, the structure of an HTML document, or the invisible architecture of a computer network — I am drawn to the underlying principles that make everything function. I approach every new concept as a puzzle worth solving completely, not just well enough to pass.

This analytical mindset extends to subjects like physics, where I actively seek advanced conceptual challenges and complex problem-solving questions. I believe that strong logical thinking is the backbone of great engineering — and I'm committed to building that foundation one concept at a time, moving deliberately from fundamentals to advanced mastery.

Outside of academics, I'm a digital content creator who enjoys connecting with people who share the same hunger for growth. My Telegram communities and YouTube channel are extensions of that same curiosity — spaces where ideas are explored openly and real progress is made together.

When I'm not studying or creating, you'll find me experimenting with technology projects, diving into music, or asking the kind of questions that don't have easy answers. I believe the best engineers are the ones who never stop being students — and I intend to be both, for life.
